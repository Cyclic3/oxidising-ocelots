Why?

Our school banned playing card games in school. We looked at a range of solutions:
  • Remote controlled robots with cameras, so we weren't technically playing in
  • Other games which could be reduced to cards through a polynomial-time reduction
  • A military coo
  • Actually do some work
We eventually settled on writing our own. This was initially supposed to be a significantly less good version of exploding kittens, our favourite card game, but we realised it would be too hard. Instead, we have ripped out all of the fun strategy for a Turing-complete flow control. And I mean turing-complete. When a player leaves, we have to reset flow, or else we have to solve the halting problem.

How?

This was carefully crafted in a beautiful flow of Rust, Go and Javascript, for safety, high speeds and code clarity. The code interfaces using high performance asynchronous socket io.

Lol jk, we hacked it together in C++ and Python, and data is passed as json over stdio because sockets are hard.

If you actually want to help, or just want to laugh at awfully indented code with more whitespace than text (Lukshan), or a C++ backend that cannot be truely understood by any sane mortal, and may actually be cursed by Satan himself (Cyclic3), visit our github.

The develop and master branches are autobuilt on push, and are pushed to the beta and production domains.
Everything is hosted on a hacked together kuberentes cluster, which is built from laptops and cobbled together bits of old computer.
